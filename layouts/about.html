About

Make It Short is a two-day short film festival covering a range of topics from all over the world. Through short films we can experience a huge range of subjects and styles, and this new independent festival includes social commentary, humour and animation. Although we are presenting work on certain themes (see below) each short film also stands alone.

This is our first Make It Short. As well as adult submissions it will include shorts created by local schoolchildren and students, and by Brazilian children as part of our cultural exchange programme.

Where and Why

Why be a part of Make It Short? It's an independent festival offering a chance to shape cultural and social debate and make contacts in the film industry. Make It Short will take place in Lewes in the South East of England, a historic and unusual town with a small population but huge political and creative influence. Well known for its history of political dissent, from the Battle of Lewes to Thomas Paine, Lewes is still a good place to meet people working in politics, literature and social change, as well as film makers of all kinds.

About the Judges

Jerry Rothwell

Jerry Rothwell is a documentary filmmaker whose work includes the award-winning feature films: How To Change The World, about the founders of Greenpeace; Town of Runners, about two girls in an Ethiopian village who aspire to be athletes; Donor Unknown, about a sperm donor and his many offspring; Heavy Load, about a group of people with learning disabilities who form a punk band, and Deep Water (co-directed with Louise Osmond), about Donald Crowhurst's ill-fated voyage in the 1968 round the world yacht race. His latest film is Sour Grapes (co-directed with Reuben Atlas) a film about a wine counterfeiter.

At Met Film Production, he has exec produced and worked as an editor on numerous feature docs including Dylan Williams' Men Who Swim and Sarah Gavron's The Village At The End Of The World.

Steve Smith

Steve Smith is a highly experienced animation director and producer, and the founder of Beakus. His work has won Annecy Crystals and a British Animation Award for Fun Facts, and a BAFTA for Newsround – Semara’s Story, whilst his clients range from the BBC to Red Bee Media, Google, McCann Erickson, Brothers and Sisters, EMI, Domino Records, Channel 4, and Nickelodeon.

An animator and filmmaker at heart Steve has made several shorts including Eating For Two (Channel 4) and Leap of Faith (MTV). In fact Leap of Faith was singled out by BAFTA as the perfect 1-minute film! His work foregrounds characterisation, humour, variety and sophistication. But perhaps his greatest skill, having produced such a diverse range of work, is to recognise the power of animation to make any subject engaging and accessible.

Abigail Norris

Abigail Norris has been an artist filmmaker for 15 years, her films explore a visual language that is emotive and psychological through observing human experience within an environment or situation. The main thread of her work focuses on the rhythms of human nature comparing it to the rhythms and patterns of nature itself. Abigail has worked with artists and creatives of varying ages and abilities. Collaborators include; visual artists, choreographers, musicians, writers, filmmakers and poets.
