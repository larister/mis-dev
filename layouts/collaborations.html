Collaborations

Brighton Film School

The Brighton Film School is helping us put together the 24 hour competition. They have also generously contributed the £100 prize.

Bob Walters

Bob was the facilitator for the short film workshop at the Wallands School. He also put us in touch with the Brazilian School, Instituto Acaia.

The Wallands School, Lewes

The school took part in a one day filming and editing workshop, where 65 children created short films about ‘A day in my life in school’, depicting their experiences spending a day at The Wallands. Martin Blount, their Head of IT, helped us by having the iPads ready for the day. The films have a creative documentary style.

Instituto Acaia, Brazil

The Instituto Acaia joined in with us creating short films with their students with the same topic as our local school: ‘A day in my life in school’. A wonderful example of cultural exchange.

Zylo

Ryan from Zylo works with children who create their own short films. He is submitting fictional short films performed by local children.

Press Play Films

Lara teachers children how to create stop motion animation; she is submitting short animations created by local children.

Sussex Downs College Media Studies Department

Dan and Anthony, who teach media studies at the local college, have helped us with fantastic volunteers for the children's workshop.

University of Brighton Media Studies Department

Various lecturers (especially Robyn Millen) from the media studies department have helped us with great volunteers for the the workshop with the children.

Other

We also had individuals who offered to help during the one day workshop, and with other duties for the festival. We are hugely grateful to them.
